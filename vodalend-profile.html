Executive Summary

VodaLend's declined applicant base is one of the most commercially valuable datasets in South Africa for debt counselling. These are credit-active, income-verified individuals who have sought credit, been assessed, and been turned down — precisely because they are already over-indebted. The profile below is calibrated to FindBetter's minimum economic threshold and converts that base into a high-quality, structured lead pipeline.

The Opportunity

A consumer who applies to VodaLend and is declined for affordability has actively sought relief, submitted to income verification, and been assessed as unable to carry more debt. The NCA over-indebtedness test is effectively already done. This is a consumer in a decision window — open to a solution and financially profiled before they arrive at FindBetter.

Economic Threshold

FindBetter's minimum viable case is a restructured monthly payment of R3,000, corresponding to total debt of approximately R150,000–R220,000. Every filter in this profile is calibrated to this threshold. Leads below it cannot generate a placed, sustainable case regardless of other characteristics.

Profile Filters

The filters below are applied in sequence. Debt load is the primary gate. Income, credit profile, and demographic filters refine the qualifying pool to maximise conversion economics.

1. Debt Load and Affordability (Primary Filter)

VariableCriteriaRationale
Total outstanding debt R150,000+ Minimum for a R3,000/month restructured payment to be economically viable.
Active credit facilities 5+ Average debt counselling applicant carries 6 facilities. 5+ reliably produces R150k+ total debt at middle-income levels.
Monthly declared debt repayments R5,000+ Fastest proxy for R150k+ total exposure if total debt not directly captured.
Debt-to-income ratio 60%+ National average DTI for debt counselling applicants is 67.3%. Below 40% consumer may still self-rescue.

2. Income Band

VariableCriteriaRationale
Monthly gross income R15,000–R60,000 Sweet spot: R18k–R40k gross. Below R15k insufficient disposable income; above R60k consumer resolves through private banking.
Employment status Salaried or verifiable self-employed Consistent monthly income required to fund restructured payment plan under NCA.
PERSAL / government payroll Preferred Employment stability high; payroll deductions enforceable; counsellors can negotiate directly with payroll.

3. Credit and Behavioural Profile

VariableCriteriaRationale
Decline reason Affordability / over-indebtedness / credit risk Strongest predictor of NCA over-indebtedness. Exclude: fraud, identity, incomplete.
Credit score 300–580 Below 300: judgements prevent debt review. Above 580: may still qualify for traditional credit. Densest band: 350–540.
Current arrears 1–3 months on 1+ facility Early-stage stress; consumer actively seeking solutions. 3+ months often signals disengagement.
Days since application Under 90 days Contact rates drop sharply after 90 days. Sweet spot: 0–30 days (active decision window).
Recent credit enquiries (6 months) 3+ Multiple credit-seeking behaviour signals over-indebtedness — consumer trying to plug a gap.
Existing debt review None Cannot be placed if already under debt review. Must exclude.

4. Demographics

VariableCriteriaRationale
Age 28–55 Peak earning and peak debt accumulation. 45+ particularly strained — 60%+ carry unsustainable debt (DebtBusters 2025).
Province National (no exclusion) Highest volumes historically: Gauteng, Western Cape, KZN.
Valid contactable mobile Yes VodaLend-verified numbers are a significant data quality advantage over cold lists.

Scorecard Variables & Yield

Matogen Scorecard — Key Predictive Variables

Our scoring partner Matogen has identified the following as the strongest predictive variables for a viable debt counselling case from a declined loan base:

2+ missed home loan repayments over a short period
Unsecured loan declined, or 3+ accounts in arrears
Debt-to-income ratio above 100%
Payday loan usage and associated defaults
High credit utilisation across facilities
Missed payments on insurance premiums
Phase 2 — Scorecard Integration

Where VodaLend captures these signals at the point of decline assessment, they can be used to rank and prioritise the lead base before it reaches FindBetter — tightening the funnel and improving conversion economics for both parties. Full API integration and automated lead routing is the target architecture for Phase 2.

Exclusion Criteria

  • Declined for fraud / identity mismatch
  • Active debt review or administration order
  • Unemployed (no verifiable income)
  • Total debt below R100,000
  • Monthly repayments below R3,500
  • Do-not-contact under POPIA
  • Under 18 years of age
